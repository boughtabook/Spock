Project Idea

My project is the development of an application for the iPad which will train people to be ambidextrous. This application would be beneficial for people who have had acquired brain injuries or have RSI injuries and their dominant hand is impaired or needs to be not used for a period of time. It could be used as a tool for elderly people with little use of their hands and be an instrument that would help in learning to use the iPad. My focus on the application would be its use of the touch screen and it could have an add on of an Apple Pencil or other applicable stylus and a keyboard.

Motivation

As someone who has experienced an acquired brain injury where I developed a weakness and lost some function to the left side of my body, my motivation in developing an iPad application is in helping people that are in rehabilitation after a stroke or brain injury to develop their co-ordination in both hands, particularly the use of the non-dominant hand if the dominant hand has been impacted by this. It would be of use to people who have RSI in their dominant hand and would give them time for their other hand to rest and recover. An estimated 27,428 Australians experienced stroke for the first time in their lives in 2020, and there were an estimated 445,087 survivors of stroke living in the community - https://strokefoundation.org.au/ In 2006 nearly half a million people in the UK suffered from some form of RSI. The problem is increasing principally through the intensive use of computers and other technology that involves large amounts of keyboarding. Posture related health problems are also growing due to the sedentary nature of many jobs. - http://www.rsi.org.uk/

Description
